# TOC Responsibilities

This document outlines the core responsibilities of the TOC and its members. Any item not defined herein is considered out of scope of the TOC.

## The TOC is a senior technical body

TOC members are the technical oversight of the projects and communities within the cloud native ecosystem. As such, our work requires us to remain abreast of shifts and changes within industry, cloud native, and open source. Each TOC member has one or more areas of expertise and experience within the technical community of the CNCF. For information on the qualifications of TOC members, please refer to section 6(d) of the Charter. As with many individual contributor roles within Industry, the operation of the TOC requires a well-rounded balance of:
* technical acumen
* leadership - often demonstrated by adherence to the [TOC Leadership Principles](PRINCIPLES.md#leadership)
* competent autonomy
* self-aware or mindful

### It is a privilege to contribute

Serving on the TOC is a grant of responsibility by the electors to an individual to fulfill the mission and vision of the TOC and the Technical Community in a technical leadership capacity. It is considered a privilege.

We serve as individuals on the TOC.  This role is in addition to our regular work and considered a selfless position. Our service is a privilege, and while it may not feel like it, the opportunity to influence the direction and execution of such a critical open source ecosystem is unparalleled. It is critical to understand that in the course of our TOC work, our organizational affiliation may restrict our ability to take on work alone however we act in the interest of the TOC and Technical Community, not in the interest of our employer

Just because we are nominated and elected to these positions does not grant those serving the right to remain. It is not a requirement nor is it an obligation to remain in service if we cannot execute the work. Every prospective and current TOC member is expected to empower and guide our projects and contributors to accomplish their goals without creating additional burden in the process. As such TOC members must continuously self-evaluate and ensure they are capable of fulfilling that grant of responsibility, to fully execute that privilege of service.

## Groups and Projects

Through the moving levels process and other activities, TOC members are responsible for coaching technical advisory groups, working groups, committees, projects, and other groups in achieving outcomes those groups define. The TOC may occassionally direct a group or project to accomplish or achieve a particular outcome, such as developing a self-assessment guide or correcting a governance oversight. TOC members are encouraged to participate in and contribute to the work of these groups but must be mindful of the capacity in which they are participating. We carry our TOC role everywhere and it holds us to a higher degree of scrutiny than our peers, therefore we are also responsible for effectively managing our participation in those activites that allows other contributors to step in and fulfill technical leadership positions and may offer guidance to enable their success in doing so.
